About Me

Student persuading a Bachelors in
Human-Centered Computing

I am currently finishing up my forth year studying Human-Centered Computing, a 4-year degree program that combines principles from computing, design, and psychology to advance the ways people interact with modern technology. I chose this major when I found out my disabilities affected my ability to use technology to its full potential. Although I have learned to operate many assistive technologies to close that gap over the years, I felt it was never quite enough. I knew I needed to do something with my life to improve access for future generations, hence my decision in choosing this degree and applying to this opening.

I currently serve as the webmaster for an on-campus Fraternity group. I help ensure the website is up to date, the information posted are correct, and in general make sure the site looks nice. I’ve been tasked to also overhaul the site, which gives me the opportunity to take greater accessibility measures. I’ve also lead a number of projects, many of which involved the relationship of how users interacts with the given platform and that it was a positive experience. Within these projects, I’ve managed as many as 5 persons in my team. Beyond being familiar in a number of assistive technologies and software, I am also well-versed in multiple programming languages, graphic design software, and operating systems.

Research Project: Icons and Labels

Research Project on the Accessibility of Icons and Labels

It’s a common misconception that users (especially mobile users) will tap around and play with all the different icons cheerfully until they discover what each icon does. In reality, users are often intimidated by new interfaces and don’t explore outside their comfort zone as much as we would like them to.
